ERWIN SCHRÖDINGER


Best known for his Wave Equation and his thought experiment known as "Schrödinger's cat."


Here`s a time line for this great scientist:

  • August 12, 1887:
    • Birth, Vienna (Austria).

  • 1910:
    • Obtained PhD in Physics, University of Vienna (Universität Wien), Vienna (Austria).

  • 1911:ç
    • Post-Doctoral Assistant to Franz Serafin Exner, University of Vienna (Universität Wien).

  • 1914 – 1918:
    • Served in the Austrian military during World War I.

  • 1920:
    • Assistant to Max Wien, University of Jena (Friedrich-Schiller-Universität Jena).

  • 1920:
    • Associate Professor, University of Stuttgart (Universität Stuttgart).

  • 1921:
    • Professor, University of Breslau, Poland (now known as Uniwersytet Wrocławski).

  • 1921 – 1927:
    • Chair of Theoretical Physics, University of Zurich (Universität Zürich), Zurich (Switzerland).

  • 1926:
    • Published famous four-part series “Annalen der Physik”.

  • 1927 – 1933:
    • Succeeded Max Planck as Chair of Theoretical Physics, Friedrich Wilhelm University of Berlin (Friedrich-Wilhelms-Universität Berlin), Berlin (Germany).

  • 1933:
    • Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics with P. A. M. Dirac "for the discovery of new productive forms of atomic theory".

  • 1933:
    • Fellow, Magdalaen College, University of Oxford, Oxford (England).

  • 1935:
    • Published “The present situation in quantum mechanics” which contains his famous Schrodinger’s Cat thought-experiment.

  • 1936 – 1939:
    • Professor of Physics, University of Graz, Austria (Universität Graz).

  • 1937:
    • Awarded the Max Planck Medal, German Physical Society (Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft).

  • 1939:
    • Escaped from Austria after it was annexed by Germany, took up temporary Professor position, University of Ghent, Belgium (Rijksuniversiteit te Gent).

  • 1940 – 1955:
    • Director, newly established Institute for Advanced Studies, Dublin (Ireland).

  • 1956:
    • Professor emeritus, University of Vienna (Universität Wien), Vienna (Austria).

  • January 4, 1961:
    • Death, Vienna (Austria).
